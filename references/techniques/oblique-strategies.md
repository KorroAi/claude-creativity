# Oblique Strategies

## When to Use
Creative block. Everything you generate feels like a variation of the same idea. You need a jolt from outside your own thought patterns. Inspired by Brian Eno and Peter Schmidt's "Oblique Strategies" card deck (1975).

## The Method
1. Acknowledge you're stuck. "Same ideas, different words."
2. Draw a random oblique strategy. Pick one or generate one:
   - "Remove the thing you're most proud of."
   - "Do the last thing first."
   - "What would your closest friend do?"
   - "Make it uglier. Then fix it."
   - "Add one unnecessary constraint."
   - "Remove the one feature everyone assumes is essential."
   - "Honor the error as a hidden intention."
   - "What are the sections of a day?"
   - "Go outside. Shut the door. Now solve it."
   - "A line has two sides. Use both."
3. Apply the chosen strategy to your problem, no matter how absurd it seems.
4. The strategy forces a perspective shift you'd never make voluntarily.
5. What emerges is often the breakthrough.

## Example
Problem: "Our dashboard has too many metrics. Users are overwhelmed."
Strategy drawn: "Remove the thing you're most proud of."
Application: Our most proud feature is the real-time graph. We spent months on it. Remove it. What remains? Just the numbers. Numbers are faster to scan. Users aren't overwhelmed anymore. The graph was the problem — we built it for ourselves, not for them.

## Why It Works
Oblique strategies bypass your conscious problem-solving and activate lateral thinking. You can't predict which card will crack the problem open, but one always does.
