# Random Stimulus

## When to Use
Fresh ideas in a new domain. Breaking out of industry echo chamber. When everyone in the room has the same background and keeps generating the same ideas.

## The Method
1. Open a random word generator, a random Wikipedia article, or pick an object in your room.
2. Describe the random stimulus in detail — its properties, behaviors, relationships.
3. Ask: "What can this teach me about my problem?"
4. Force at least 3 connections. The first one will be surface-level. Push deeper.
5. Extract the structural principle and apply it.

## Example
Problem: "How do we improve our onboarding flow?"
Random stimulus: A river.
Properties: Water flows downhill (path of least resistance). Tributaries join the main flow. Rocks create eddies and turbulence. Rivers carve canyons over time.
Connections:
- Path of least resistance → Remove all friction from onboarding. Every extra field is a rock.
- Tributaries → Users come from different sources. Each needs its own entry flow that joins the main experience.
- Carving over time → The first experience shapes user behavior permanently. Get the first 30 seconds right.
Applied: Single-field signup → personalized onboarding river based on source → obsessive focus on the first interaction.

## Why It Works
Serendipity is a creativity engine. Random stimuli force your brain to make connections it would never make voluntarily, which is exactly how breakthrough ideas form.
