# Synthesis

## When to Use
When you have two competing ideas and can't choose. When the team is split between Approach A and Approach B. When the real answer is neither — it's the third thing.

## The Method
1. Clearly articulate Idea A and Idea B. What do they optimize for? What do they sacrifice?
2. Ask: "What is the THIRD thing? Not A. Not B. Not a compromise. A genuine synthesis."
3. Find what A and B share — the common goal, the hidden assumption, the deeper need.
4. Build a solution that achieves the shared goal without inheriting the trade-offs of either.
5. The synthesis should feel inevitable in hindsight: "Of course. It was never A or B."

## Example
Idea A: "We should build a mobile app — everyone's on their phone."
Idea B: "We should build a web app — no install friction."
A optimizes for: mobile reach. Sacrifices: install friction, cross-platform dev cost.
B optimizes for: zero friction, one codebase. Sacrifices: mobile-native feel.
Shared goal: Reach users on their phones with minimal friction.
Synthesis: Progressive Web App that feels native. One codebase. Install button optional — works in browser first, becomes an "app" when users choose. Best of both, trade-offs of neither.

## Why It Works
Most either/or debates are false dichotomies. The tension between A and B contains energy that, when harnessed, creates something genuinely new — not a compromise, but a breakthrough.
