# Creative Constraints

## When to Use
Too many options. Analysis paralysis. Everything seems possible so nothing gets chosen. Need to force focus.

## The Method
1. Identify the problem space.
2. Impose an arbitrary, severe constraint. Make it absurd at first.
3. Design a solution within that constraint. Take it seriously.
4. Notice what the constraint reveals about the problem.
5. Relax the constraint to a useful level, keeping the insight.

## Example
Problem: "Design the homepage for our SaaS product."
Constraint: "You can only use 50 words total. No images. Black and white only."
Within constraint: Every word must fight for its life. The value proposition must be one sentence. Navigation must be 1-2 items max.
Revelation: The constraint forces you to identify the ONE thing that matters most. Most homepages are cluttered because nobody imposed a limit.
Relaxed outcome: Keep the ruthless prioritization. Allow color and images only where they directly support the core message.

## Why It Works
Constraints force creativity by eliminating the comfort of "I'll just add more." Scarcity drives focus; focus drives insight.
