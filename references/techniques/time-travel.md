# Time Travel

## When to Use
When short-term thinking has you stuck. When you're optimizing for now and forgetting that time changes everything. When you need perspective that only decades can provide.

## The Method
1. State today's problem clearly.
2. Travel backward: "How would I solve this in 2016?" With the tools they had, the constraints they accepted, the assumptions they couldn't see. What would 2016-you do that 2026-you is too sophisticated to consider?
3. Travel forward: "How will someone solve this in 2036?" With tools we don't have yet, constraints that don't exist, assumptions that will look ridiculous. What seems impossible today that will be obvious tomorrow?
4. Extract the timeless insight between the two extremes.
5. Apply it today.

## Example
Problem: "Our app needs authentication but every auth solution is a pain."
2016 solution: Roll your own. bcrypt, session cookies, custom UI. Painful but 100% control.
2036 solution: No auth at all. Devices are biometric. Your phone IS your identity. Apps authenticate passively — you don't even notice.
Timeless insight: Auth friction is temporary. Build for the future where auth disappears. Today: passkeys. Tomorrow: nothing. "Sign in with Apple" → "Already signed in, it's literally your hands holding the phone."

## Why It Works
Time amplifies constraints and reveals what's truly essential. What survives 20 years of change is the real problem. Everything else is fashion.
