# Forced Analogies

## When to Use
Design, UX, naming, branding, product features. When you need fresh perspectives on something familiar.

## The Method
1. Define what you're designing or naming.
2. Pick a completely unrelated domain (nature, music, cooking, architecture, sports).
3. Map your problem onto that domain: "If this feature were a dish, what would it be?"
4. Extract structural parallels, not surface-level ones.
5. Translate the insight back to your domain.

## Example
Task: Name a new fast database query library.
Domain: Cooking.
"If this library were a kitchen tool, what would it be?" → A mandoline slicer — fast, precise, minimal effort, dangerous if misused.
Structural parallel: Speed + precision + minimal setup.
Name ideas: Mandoline, Slice, RazorQuery, SlicerDB.

## Why It Works
Analogical thinking is the core of creative insight. Forced cross-domain mapping bypasses your brain's usual categorization, revealing structures you wouldn't otherwise see.
